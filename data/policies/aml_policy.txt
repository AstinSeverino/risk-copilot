ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) POLICY — Merchant Monitoring

1. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
   - Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) — 31 USC §5311 et seq.
   - USA PATRIOT Act — Title III
   - FinCEN regulations — 31 CFR Chapter X
   - EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive (AMLD6) — Directive 2018/1673
   - FATF Recommendations (updated 2023)

2. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY INDICATORS FOR MERCHANTS
   2.1 Volume-Based Indicators
       - Sudden unexplained increase in transaction volume (>5x baseline)
       - Transaction volume inconsistent with business type/size
       - Rapid growth without corresponding business expansion evidence

   2.2 Pattern-Based Indicators
       - Round-dollar transactions (structuring indicator)
       - Transactions just below reporting thresholds ($10,000 CTR)
       - Burst patterns: high volume followed by dormancy
       - Night/weekend transactions inconsistent with business hours

   2.3 Customer-Based Indicators
       - Low customer diversity (few customers, many transactions)
       - Same customer making many small transactions (smurfing)
       - High percentage of declined/reversed transactions

   2.4 Geographic Indicators
       - High foreign card percentage for domestic business
       - Transactions from FATF high-risk jurisdictions
       - Cross-border patterns inconsistent with business model

3. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD) TRIGGERS
   - Any merchant with risk score > 0.8 for more than 3 consecutive days
   - Merchants in high-risk MCCs: 7995 (Gambling), 5993 (Tobacco), 5944 (Jewelry)
   - Merchants with unresolved KYB flags
   - Merchants with prior SAR filings

4. SAR FILING CRITERIA
   - Mandatory filing when: confirmed or suspected money laundering activity
   - Filing deadline: within 30 calendar days of detection
   - Dollar threshold: $5,000 aggregate in suspicious transactions
   - No threshold: when transaction involves known criminal activity
   - SAR narrative must include: who, what, when, where, why, how

5. RECORD RETENTION
   - Transaction records: minimum 5 years (BSA requirement)
   - SAR filings: minimum 5 years from filing date
   - Investigation records: 7 years (AMLD6 requirement)
   - Agent reasoning traces: 7 years (audit trail for AI-assisted decisions)
   - Model versions and parameters: retained for full model lifecycle

6. RISK-BASED APPROACH
   - Low risk (score < 0.3): standard monitoring, batch review
   - Medium risk (0.3-0.7): enhanced monitoring, weekly review
   - High risk (0.7-0.9): daily review, EDD within 30 days
   - Critical risk (> 0.9): immediate review, potential processing hold

7. ESCALATION PROCEDURES
   - Level 1 (Automated): Risk scoring and auto-approve for low-risk
   - Level 2 (AI-Assisted): Agent investigation with human review
   - Level 3 (Analyst Review): Senior analyst manual investigation
   - Level 4 (Compliance Officer): SAR filing decision
   - Level 5 (Law Enforcement): Direct referral for critical cases

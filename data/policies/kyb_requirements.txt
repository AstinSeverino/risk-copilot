KNOW YOUR BUSINESS (KYB) VERIFICATION POLICY

1. INITIAL ONBOARDING VERIFICATION
   1.1 Required Documents
       - Business registration / articles of incorporation
       - EIN (Employer Identification Number) or equivalent tax ID
       - Government-issued ID of all beneficial owners (>25% ownership)
       - Proof of business address (utility bill, lease agreement)
       - Bank account verification (micro-deposit or bank letter)

   1.2 Verification Checks
       - Business registry validation (Secretary of State records)
       - Beneficial owner identity verification (KYC)
       - Sanctions screening: OFAC SDN List, EU Consolidated List, UN Security Council
       - PEP screening: Politically Exposed Persons database
       - Adverse media screening: negative news, legal proceedings
       - MCC code validation: declared business matches actual operations

   1.3 Risk-Based Tiering at Onboarding
       - Standard risk: retail, restaurants, grocery (MCC 5411, 5812, 5541)
       - Elevated risk: e-commerce, digital goods, travel agencies
       - High risk: gambling (7995), adult content, cryptocurrency, money services
       - Prohibited: illegal activities, unlicensed financial services

2. ONGOING MONITORING
   2.1 Periodic Re-verification
       - Standard risk: annual KYB refresh
       - Elevated risk: semi-annual KYB refresh
       - High risk: quarterly KYB refresh
       - Trigger-based: re-verify on material business changes

   2.2 Continuous Monitoring Triggers
       - Ownership change or beneficial owner change
       - Business address change
       - MCC code change request
       - Volume exceeding tier limits
       - Sanctions list updates (daily check)
       - Adverse media alerts

3. BUSINESS AGE RISK ASSESSMENT
   - < 30 days: highest risk tier, maximum processing limits apply
   - 30-90 days: elevated risk, enhanced monitoring
   - 90-180 days: standard monitoring with review at 180 days
   - 180-365 days: establishing history, reduced monitoring
   - > 365 days: established merchant, standard monitoring

4. MCC-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
   4.1 Gambling (MCC 7995)
       - Mandatory EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence)
       - Licensing verification required
       - Transaction monitoring: daily review
       - Volume caps until established (90-day probation)
       - Jurisdictional compliance check

   4.2 Restaurants/Food Service (MCC 5812)
       - Standard KYB sufficient
       - Monitor for card-present to card-not-present shifts
       - Seasonal volume variations expected and documented

   4.3 Gas Stations (MCC 5541)
       - Standard KYB plus fuel license verification
       - Pay-at-pump fraud monitoring
       - Pre-authorization amount monitoring

   4.4 Miscellaneous Retail (MCC 5999)
       - Enhanced monitoring due to broad category
       - Verify actual merchandise matches declared business
       - Cross-check with online presence

5. FLAG CATEGORIES
   - SANCTIONS_HIT: Match found on sanctions list — immediate BLOCK
   - PEP_MATCH: Beneficial owner is politically exposed — enhanced review
   - BUSINESS_TOO_NEW: Registration < 30 days — volume restrictions
   - MCC_HIGH_RISK: Declared MCC in high-risk category — EDD required
   - DOCUMENT_EXPIRED: KYB documents past renewal date — re-verification
   - ADDRESS_MISMATCH: Business address doesn't match records — investigate

6. DECISION IMPACT ON RISK SCORING
   - KYB PASS: no impact on risk score
   - KYB PASS with flags: +0.1 risk score adjustment
   - KYB FAIL: automatic BLOCK recommendation
   - KYB PENDING: automatic REVIEW recommendation
   - KYB flags feed into reason codes: KYB_FLAG, BUSINESS_AGE_CONCERN

TRANSACTION LAUNDERING DETECTION POLICY

1. DEFINITION
   Transaction laundering (also called factoring or payment laundering) occurs when a
   merchant processes payment card transactions on behalf of another business, or when
   the true nature of the goods/services being sold is disguised.

2. KEY INDICATORS
   2.1 Low Customer Diversity
       - Customer-to-transaction ratio below 0.3
       - Same card numbers appearing repeatedly across short timeframes
       - Transactions from few unique BINs (bank identification numbers)
       - Risk level: HIGH when combined with volume anomaly

   2.2 Card-Not-Present (CNP) Anomalies
       - Sudden shift from card-present to card-not-present transactions
       - CNP ratio increase > 20 percentage points vs baseline
       - CNP transactions at traditionally card-present MCCs (restaurants, gas stations)
       - Risk level: MEDIUM-HIGH, investigate merchant's online presence

   2.3 Ticket Size Manipulation
       - Transactions clustered just below floor limits
       - Uniform transaction amounts (indicates automated processing)
       - Average ticket dramatically different from MCC norms
       - Split transactions to avoid monitoring thresholds

   2.4 Temporal Anomalies
       - Transactions outside normal business hours
       - Uniform distribution across hours (human behavior shows peaks)
       - Weekend/holiday volume equal to weekday volume
       - Measured by hour entropy metric (low entropy = suspicious clustering)

   2.5 Velocity Patterns
       - Transaction rate exceeding physical POS terminal capacity
       - More than 1 transaction per second on single terminal
       - Volume growth ratio > 10x without business expansion evidence

3. SCORING METHODOLOGY
   - Each indicator contributes to a laundering risk sub-score
   - Combined via weighted sum: customer_diversity (30%), cnp_pattern (25%),
     ticket_anomaly (20%), temporal_pattern (15%), velocity (10%)
   - Sub-score > 0.7: flag for investigation
   - Sub-score > 0.9: recommend BLOCK

4. INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES
   4.1 Initial Assessment (Automated)
       - Feature extraction: customer_txn_ratio, pct_card_present, hour_entropy
       - Peer comparison against same-MCC merchants
       - Web search for merchant online presence verification

   4.2 Deep Investigation (Analyst-Assisted)
       - Verify merchant website matches declared business
       - Check for multiple merchants at same physical address
       - Review merchant's acquiring bank history
       - Check for undisclosed affiliations or common ownership

   4.3 Decision Framework
       - APPROVE: Anomaly explained by legitimate business changes
       - REVIEW: Suspicious patterns but insufficient evidence for BLOCK
       - BLOCK: Strong evidence of laundering, initiate merchant termination process

5. FALSE POSITIVE MITIGATION
   - Legitimate causes of low customer diversity:
     * B2B merchants with few large clients
     * Subscription-based businesses
     * Seasonal businesses with repeat customers
   - Legitimate causes of CNP increase:
     * Merchant launching e-commerce channel
     * COVID-era shift to online ordering
     * Mobile payment adoption
   - Always check for external context before escalating

6. REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS
   - Card brand rules: Visa VAMP, Mastercard MATCH
   - Acquirer liability for merchant monitoring
   - SAR filing when laundering is suspected
   - Cooperation with law enforcement upon subpoena
